Frequently Asked Questions

1. What do you need to know about the Special Resident Retiree's VISA (SRRVISA)?
The Special Resident Retiree's Visa (SRRV) is a non-immigrant multiple-entry, indefinite-stay visa. With the SRRV, the holders may stay in the Philippines for as long as they want without giving up their citizenship. It is the core product of the Philippine Retirement Authority (PRA), a government-owned and controlled corporation mandated to attract foreign nationals and former Filipino citizens to make the Philippines their second home or retirement destination.
2. Who can apply?
Foreign Nationals or Former Filipino Citizens who are 50 years old and above
For Dependents:
Spouse: Legally married to Principal Retiree
Children: Legitimate or legally adopted, unmarried, and below 21 years old upon joining the program.
